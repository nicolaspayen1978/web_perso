Genesis

The 4Gs portfolio was born out of a desire to align my investments with long-term societal and technological transformations while maintaining a disciplined, value-driven approach. As a growth value investor with a slightly contrarian mindset, I sought to balance innovation, impact, and resilience in my portfolio. Over time, I identified four key themes—Gray (aging population), Green (climate tech), Gratification/Generational (entertainment & lifestyle), and Global (geographical diversification)—each reflecting my interests, experience, and conviction in structural shifts shaping the world. The 4Gs framework allows me to invest in high-growth opportunities while ensuring sustainability, diversification, and alignment with my core investment principles.

Investment thesis

The 4Gs Portfolio is built on the belief that long-term wealth creation comes from investing in transformative sectors that shape the future while maintaining a disciplined approach to valuation. As a growth-value investor with a slightly contrarian mindset, I seek companies positioned in high-growth markets but at fair valuations, avoiding speculative excesses.

My portfolio focuses on four key themes:

  • Gray (Healthcare & Aging): Companies addressing the needs of an aging population.
  • Green (Climate Tech & Energy Transition): Businesses driving sustainability and the energy transition.
  • Gratification & Generational (Entertainment, Lifestyle & Cultural Icons): This category includes companies that define and evolve with each generation—from Gen W to Gen Alpha—shaping consumer behavior, culture, and technological adoption.
  • Global (Diversified Geographic Exposure): Investing beyond borders, focusing on the company’s fundamentals rather than its country of origin.

I prioritize businesses with strong fundamentals, track record of shareholder performances, competitive advantages (leadership position), and sustainable impact, balancing growth potential with resilience to navigate market cycles. The Generational aspect of the portfolio acknowledges that every era has its defining companies—whether in media, technology, fashion, or consumer products—and aims to capture those shaping the identity and preferences of each generation.

Investment process

My investment process combines fundamental analysis, market screening, and strategic portfolio management. I identify companies with strong fundamentals, competitive advantages, and fair valuations, leveraging financial reports, earnings calls, and industry research. To refine my stock selection and timing, I use data-driven analysis through my Python-based KOI libraries, which help assess valuation, risk-adjusted returns, and momentum indicators like RSI. My portfolio is actively monitored and rebalanced, with a focus on long-term growth, dividend reinvestment, and sectoral shifts to align with evolving market trends and macroeconomic conditions.
